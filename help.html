What is ProChorder?

Your complete on-stage music tool

ProChorder is a web-based application for musicians that lets you write, organize, and perform songs with chords and lyrics — all in one place. It works on any device with a browser, and can be installed as a Progressive Web App (PWA) so it works even without an internet connection.

Quick Start

Up and running in under 5 minutes

1

Open ProChorder

Visit the app in your browser or install it to your home screen via your browser's "Add to Home Screen" option for the best offline experience.

2

Sign In with Google

Tap the hamburger menu (☰) → Sign in with Google. This enables cloud sync and band collaboration. You can use the app without signing in, but your songs will only be saved locally.

3

Create Your First Song

Tap + New Song from the song selector. Give your song a title, then tap EDIT to enter edit mode and start adding chords and lyrics.

4

Save Your Song

Open the side menu → Save Song. Your song is saved locally and synced to the cloud if you're signed in.

5

Invite Your Band

Open the menu → Band → Create new band. Share the invite code with your bandmates so they can join and see all songs in real-time.

Songs & Library

Managing your song collection

Creating a New Song

Open the side menu and tap New Song. ProChorder will generate a unique name automatically (e.g. "Untitled song", "Untitled song 1") to prevent overwriting existing songs. You can rename it at any time by tapping the title field and saving.

Renaming a Song

Enter Edit mode → tap the song title at the top of the editor and type a new name → Save the song. ProChorder checks that no other song has the same name before saving.

Deleting a Song

Open the side menu → select the song in the dropdown → tap Delete Song. You'll be asked to confirm once. The song is deleted from both your device and the cloud (including for all band members).

⚠️

Heads up: Deleting a song in Band Mode removes it for every member of the band immediately. There is no undo.

Navigating Between Songs

Use the song selector dropdown in the top bar, or swipe left/right on the song area (swiping is disabled in Edit mode). You can also reorder songs in the library by dragging the ≡ handle in the dropdown list.

🎸

Chord Editor

Building and editing chords

ProChorder uses an inline chord-over-lyric format. Chords appear directly above the word or syllable they belong to.

— Verse —
D      D/C                 A
Oh Father high in Heaven
D      D/C                       G
smile down upon your son, yeah!

Adding a Chord

1

Enter Edit Mode

Tap the EDIT button in the top-right corner. The editor becomes interactive.

2

Select Chord Mode

Make sure Chord mode is active in the floating toolbar (not Text mode).

3

Click on a Chord Line

Tap the chord row above a lyric line. The Chord Builder opens with a radial selector for root note, chord type, and bass note.

4

Build Your Chord

Select root (A–G#), type (maj, min, 7, maj7, sus4, dim, aug…) and optional bass note for slash chords like D/C. Tap Insert to place the chord.

Editing or Removing a Chord

In Edit mode, tap any existing chord to open the Chord Builder with the chord pre-filled. Change the values and tap Update, or tap Remove to delete the chord.

Chord Types Available

🎵 Standard

  • Major (no suffix) — C, D, G
  • Minor — Cm, Dm, Gm
  • Dominant 7th — C7, G7
  • Minor 7th — Cm7
  • Major 7th — Cmaj7

🎵 Extended & Altered

  • 6th, m6 — C6, Cm6
  • 9th, m9 — C9, Cm9
  • 11th, m11 — C11, Cm11
  • Sus4, dim, aug
  • Slash chords — D/C, G/B
💡

Tip: You can type links directly in text mode using the format [[https://youtube.com|Watch video]] or just paste a URL and press Space — it auto-converts to a clickable link.

Sections

Organizing your song structure

Sections divide your song into labeled blocks — Intro, Verse, Chorus, Bridge, Solo, etc. They make it easy to navigate during rehearsal and performance.

Inserting a Section

Open the side menu → Sections → choose a section type from the dropdown → tap Insert Section. The section label is inserted at the current cursor position in the editor.

Available section types: Intro · Verse · Chorus · Stick · Bridge · Solo · Outro · Dig

Moving Sections

In Edit mode, each section block has ↑ ↓ buttons to move the entire section (including its content) up or down. Use the copy button to duplicate a section.

🎼

Transpose

Shift all chords up or down

Transpose lets you shift every chord in the song up or down by one semitone at a time, without needing to edit each chord individually.

Open the side menu → Transpose. Use the ▲ Up and ▼ Down buttons. Changes are applied live. Save the song to keep the new key.

ℹ️

Note: Transposing uses sharps (C#, D#…) by default. If you prefer flats (Db, Eb…), you can manually edit the individual chords after transposing.

Live Mode

Auto-scroll for hands-free performance

Live Mode scrolls the song automatically so you can keep your eyes on the audience — not your fingers. Set the scroll speed to match your song's tempo and duration.

Starting Live Mode

1

Set Duration & Speed

Use the duration inputs (minutes:seconds) or the speed slider to control how fast the song scrolls. They're linked — changing one updates the other.

2

Tap the Live Button

Tap the floating ▶ Live button at the bottom of the screen to show the scroll controls bar.

3

Play / Pause

Tap the ▶ / ⏸ button to start or pause scrolling. Use ◀ ▶ to jump to the previous or next song in your setlist.

💡

Screen Stay-Awake: Live Mode activates a Wake Lock to prevent your screen from turning off mid-performance (supported on most modern mobile browsers).

📝

Setlists (Gig Mode)

Build and share your set for the night

Creating a Setlist

Open the side menu → Create Setlist. Check the songs you want to include (in the order you want to play them), then tap Generate Setlist Code. Share the code with your bandmates.

Loading a Setlist (Gig Mode)

Open the side menu → Load Setlist → enter the code. The app enters Gig Mode — your library is filtered to show only the setlist songs in order, and the song selector turns red to indicate you're in Gig Mode.

Tap Exit Gig Mode in the top bar to return to your full library.

🥁

Metronome

Keep perfect time

Each song stores its own BPM setting. The metronome uses the Web Audio API for precise, low-latency timing.

Find the BPM input and ▲ ▼ buttons in the Live controls bar at the bottom. Tap the metronome icon to start/stop. The BPM is saved with the song.

🎻

Tuner

Built-in chromatic tuner

Open the side menu → Tuner. The tuner uses your device's microphone to detect pitch in real time. Grant microphone permission when prompted.

ℹ️

Note: Microphone access is required. The tuner works best in a quiet environment and with the device held close to your instrument.

Creating a Band

Sign in with Google first. Open the menu → Band → Create new band. Enter a band name and you'll receive a unique invite code. Share this code with your bandmates.

Joining a Band

Open the menu → Band → Join band → enter the invite code. All band songs become instantly available.

Leaving a Band

Open the menu → Band → Leave Band. You'll return to your personal song library. Band songs are not deleted from the band's shared library.

⚠️

Important: Songs and changes made in Band Mode are shared with all members in real-time. Deleting a song removes it for everyone in the band.

☁️

Cloud Sync

Your songs, everywhere

When signed in with Google, all your songs are automatically synced to Firebase. Changes made on one device appear on all others within seconds.

✅ What syncs

  • Song content (chords & lyrics)
  • Song order in the library
  • Title, artist, BPM, font size
  • Scroll speed and duration
  • Band song deletions and renames

❌ What doesn't sync

  • Dark/light mode preference
  • Font size display setting
  • Setlist codes (share manually)
  • Unsaved changes (save first!)

Export & Import

Backup, share, and migrate your library

Export Options

📄

PDF

Export the current song as a clean, printable PDF with chords and lyrics.

📝

TXT

Plain text export — useful for sharing with people who don't use ProChorder.

📦

ZIP

Export all songs as individual text files in a single ZIP archive.

🗄

JSON

Full backup of one or all songs in JSON format — perfect for restoring later.

Importing Songs

Open the side menu → Import → tap Choose File and select a .json file previously exported from ProChorder. The songs are merged into your existing library.

Tips & Tricks

Get more out of ProChorder

💡

Install as App: In Chrome or Safari, use "Add to Home Screen" to install ProChorder as a PWA. It loads faster, works offline, and feels like a native app.

💡

Reorder your library: Drag the ≡ handle next to any song name in the dropdown to reorder your library. The order syncs to the cloud for all band members.

💡

Quick save while editing: Save often! ProChorder doesn't auto-save. Get into the habit of saving after every significant change, especially before a gig.

ℹ️

Service Worker updates: When a new version of ProChorder is deployed, the app will update automatically in the background. Refresh the page once after an update to get the latest features.

💡

C/T toggle: Use the C/T button in the chord panel to switch between Chord and Text input mode without leaving the editor.
